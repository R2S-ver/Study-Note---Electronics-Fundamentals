CHAPTER 02

🟪 Voltage and Current

🟦 1. The Water Analogy
  • Voltage (V): Water pressure.
  • Current (I): Flow rate.
  • Resistance (R): Pipe thickness.
Ohm's Law: $V = I \times R$
🟦 2. Complex Explanation: Field vs Electrons

Electrons move at a "snail's pace" (drift velocity), but energy propagates at nearly the speed of light via the electromagnetic field.

Voltage is the spatial accumulation of electric field force:

$$V = \int_{A}^{B} \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}$$
🟦 3. Kirchhoff’s Laws

KCL (Conservation of Charge): $\sum I_{in} = \sum I_{out}$

KVL (Conservation of Energy): $\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = 0$

🟦 Resistor

A resistor creates resistance in current flow. Most common is the Carbon Film Resistor.

Case Study - Voltage Dropping: To power a 3V LED from 5V at 13.5mA:

R = (5V - 3V) / 0.0135A = 148Ω (Use 150Ω)
